Cacao refers to the pod and beans derived from the chocolate tree, Theobroma cacao.
Cocoa is used once the beans have been roasted and turned into nibs and powder, which are then used chocolate bars and other products.

Once Upon A Time

The cacao plant was first given its botanical name by Swedish natural scientist Carl Linnaeus, where he called it Theobroma Cacao, meaning food of the gods in Greek. Human usage of cocoa beans can be traced back to 4,000 years ago. Long before Richard Cadbury first put chocolates in a heart-shaped box for Valentine's day, it was an important part of Mayan wedding rituals. The bride and groom would exchange hot chocolate beverages during the ceremony, this tradition foreshadows chocolate's future status as a universal expression of love.
